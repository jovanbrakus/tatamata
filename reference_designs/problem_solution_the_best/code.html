Problem Statement

Given vector a = (2, -1, 3) and b = (4, 2, 1), find the scalar projection of a onto b.

Hard Vectors

Key Formula: Scalar Projection

compba = a · b / |b|

The scalar projection of vector a onto vector b is the length of the shadow of a cast onto b. It is obtained by the dot product divided by the magnitude of the target vector.

Step 01: Calculate Dot Product

First, we calculate the dot product of a · b.
(2)(4) + (-1)(2) + (3)(1) = 8 - 2 + 3 = 9

Step 02: Calculate Magnitude of b

Next, find the magnitude |b|.
√(4² + 2² + 1²) = √(16 + 4 + 1) = √21

Step 03: Apply Formula

Finally, divide the dot product by the magnitude.
9 / √21
